The RBT met with Bradley and Grandmother at home to implement program targets. There have been no environmental changes recently.

Upon arrival, Bradley was engaged in free play with toy cars in the living room. The RBT provided a clear instruction to place the toys in the storage bin. After a pause of approximately 15 seconds, Bradley was prompted to follow the instruction. He responded by throwing several toy cars across the floor and stating, "I don't want to do this," while turning his body away from the RBT. During this activity, Bradley manifested Task Refusal by not complying with the instruction after the initial prompt and Property Destruction by forcefully throwing multiple toy cars onto the floor. To address these behaviors, the RBT implemented Differential Reinforcement of Alternative Behaviors (DRA), reinforcing Bradley for placing toys in the bin when compliance occurred. Following these interventions, Bradley exhibited a reduction in Task Refusal and Property Destruction and completed the assigned task. Additionally, the RBT implemented the replacement program "Follow instruction 5-7 seconds after being given instruction" by providing clear, concise directions and systematically increasing wait times before prompts to encourage independent compliance within the specified time frame.

Later during the session, Bradley was engaged in coloring with markers at the art table when he was informed that coloring time was finished and it was time to transition to a puzzle activity on the floor. Upon this transition demand, Bradley threw multiple markers across the room and knocked over the marker container. This Property Destruction manifested as forcefully throwing multiple markers and overturning the marker container onto the floor. To address this behavior, the RBT applied the Premack Principle by allowing Bradley access to a preferred activity contingent upon completing the transition to the puzzle task. Following this intervention, Bradley exhibited a reduction in Property Destruction and participated in the transition. The RBT implemented the replacement program "Participate in transitions from preferred to non-preferred activities." by providing clear transition warnings, offering choices when possible, and reinforcing successful transitions with verbal praise and preferred items.

Throughout the session, the RBT used various reinforcers, including verbal praise (e.g., "Good job," "Wow," and "Good attention to detail"), preferred toys, and videos contingent on task completion and appropriate behavior. There were no health or safety concerns during the visit. The RBT will continue working with the client as outlined in the Behavior Plan. All data on maladaptive behaviors and progress in program implementation was collected during the session in accordance with the BIP. The session was completed as planned, with the caregiver present during implementation.

Bradley's performance during the session was fair.
