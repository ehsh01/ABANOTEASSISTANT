# Instructions for writing ABA session notes (ABC format)

*Converted from source RTF for this repository.*

When writing session notes using the **ABC format (Antecedent – Behavior – Consequence)**, it is important to understand how **Skill Acquisition Goals and Replacement Programs** should be documented.

Skill Acquisition Goals and Replacement Programs are **essentially the same targets from the treatment plan**. The difference is **where they appear in the ABC description depending on how they are being used during the session.**

There are **two correct ways** to mention them in a session note.

## 1. When the skill/replacement program is the activity being taught

In some cases, the skill acquisition goal or replacement program is **part of the activity the RBT is implementing**. In this situation, it should be mentioned **at the beginning of the ABC description**, before the maladaptive behavior occurs.

This means the goal explains **what the RBT was trying to teach or practice during the activity.**

**Example:** While working on the goal **"Stay on task for at least 3–5 minutes,"** the RBT used a visual timer set to three minutes and provided verbal prompts to maintain the client's attention on a worksheet activity involving number lines and counting bears. During this task, the client engaged in task refusal by turning his head away from the materials and not responding to the instruction after the initial prompt.

In this case, the goal is part of the **Antecedent**, because it describes the teaching activity.

## 2. When the replacement program is used after a maladaptive behavior

In other cases, the replacement program is mentioned **after a maladaptive behavior occurs**, because the RBT is teaching or reinforcing an appropriate alternative behavior that serves the same function.

In this situation, the replacement program should appear **in the consequence/intervention section** of the ABC description.

**Example:** During the activity, the client engaged in bolting by running away from the designated play area toward the fence. The RBT implemented response blocking and redirected the client back to the activity area. The RBT then reinforced the replacement behavior **"Follow demands after the first prompt provided"** when the client returned to the activity and waited for his turn appropriately.

In this case, the replacement program appears **after the behavior**, because it is the appropriate behavior being reinforced.

## Important rule

Within **one ABC scenario**, you should **only use one of these approaches.**

Do **not** describe the same skill both as the activity being taught and as the replacement behavior in the same ABC paragraph.

Choose **one** of the following for each ABC scenario:

- The goal is the **activity being implemented (Antecedent)**  
  **or**
- The goal is the **replacement behavior reinforced after the maladaptive behavior (Consequence)**

Both approaches are correct, but **they should not be used together** in the same ABC description.

## Additional documentation expectations

When writing each ABC scenario, make sure to clearly describe:

**Antecedent**

- What activity was occurring
- Instructions given by the RBT
- Materials or environment involved

**Behavior**

- The maladaptive behavior observed
- Objective description of what the client did
- Avoid opinions or interpretations

**Consequence / intervention**

- The strategies the RBT used to address the behavior
- Teaching procedures or behavior interventions implemented
- The client's response to the intervention
- If applicable, the replacement behavior reinforced

Always ensure the note reflects **what actually occurred during the session and aligns with the client's behavior plan.**
